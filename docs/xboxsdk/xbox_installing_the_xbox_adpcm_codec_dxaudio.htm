Installing the Xbox ADPCM Codec

This articles covers the installation of the Xbox Adaptive Delta Pulse Code Modulation (ADPCM) codec and making it accessible to your favorite sound recording tools.

  • Installation Steps
  • Accessing the Codec
  • Related Topics

Installation Steps

The Xbox ADCPM codec is available for download from the Xbox ADPCM section of the Xbox Development Tools page on Xbox Central. ADPCM.EXE is a self-extracting executable file. Once you've downloaded ADPCM.EXE, complete the following steps to install the Xbox ADPCM codec on your system:

  1. Run ADPCM.EXE.
  2. Select a directory to use for decompressing the files (the default is C:\ADPCM).
  3. Unzip the archive.
  4. In the folder where the files are extracted, right-click on the file XBADPCM.INF and choose "Install" from the context menu.

At this point, the Xbox ADPCM codec is installed.

Accessing the Codec

A known issue with accessing the Xbox ADPCM codec is a bug in the Voxware codec that prevents the proper enumeration of any codecs installed after the Voxware codec. Chances are the Voxware codec will have already been installed on your system (for example, by Microsoft DirectX) before the Xbox ADPCM codec, so you will need complete one of the following options to enable the Xbox ADPCM codec:

  • Lower the priority of the Voxware codec. This will allow applications that use this codec to continue to function properly, while allowing you to access the Xbox ADPCM codec. If you install any additional codecs, you may need to lower the priority of the Voxware codec again to access the new additions. For the steps to complete this option, see Lowering the priority of the Voxware Codec.
  • Disable the Voxware codec. This will allow you to access the Xbox ADPCM codec, but will affect any applications that require the Voxware codec. Since the codec will still be installed on your system, you can always enable the Voxware codec when needed. For the steps to complete this option, see Disabling the Voxware codec.
  • Remove the Voxware codec. This will allow you to access the Xbox ADPCM codec, but will affect any applications that require the Voxware codec. For the steps to complete this option, see Removing the Voxware Codec.

Lowering the priority of the Voxware Codec

Complete the following steps to lower the priority of the Voxware codec.

  1. From the Windows desktop, click on Start to bring up the Start menu.
  2. Click on Settings to bring up the Settings sub-menu.
  3. Click on Control Panel, to open the Control Panel.
  4. Double-click on Sounds and Multimedia to bring up the Sounds and Multimedia Properties dialog box.
  5. Click on the Hardware tab.
  6. Double-click on the Audio codecs option in the Devices list. This will bring up the Audio codecs Properties dialog box.
  7. Click on the Properties tab. This will bring up the Audio Compression codecs list.
  8. Double-click on the Voxware Compression Toolkit to bring up the Voxware Compression Toolkit Properties dialog box.
  9. Click the arrow next to the number where it says Change priority from <some number> to <some number>. This will give you a drop down list box of all of the possible priorities for the codec.
  10. Scroll down to the bottom of the list and select the last number.
  11. Click OK to close the Voxware Compression Toolkit Properties dialog box.
  12. Click OK to close the Audio codecs Properties dialog box.
  13. Click OK to close the Sounds and Multimedia Properties dialog box.

Disabling the Voxware Codec

Complete the following steps to disable the Voxware Codec.

  1. From the Windows desktop, click on Start to bring up the Start menu.
  2. Click on Settings to bring up the Settings sub-menu.
  3. Click on Control Panel, to open the Control Panel.
  4. Double-click on Sounds and Multimedia to bring up the Sounds and Multimedia Properties dialog box.
  5. Click on the Hardware tab.
  6. Double-click on the Audio codecs option in the Devices list. This will bring up the Audio codecs Properties dialog box.
  7. Click on the Properties tab. This will bring up the Audio Compression codecs list.
  8. Double-click on the Voxware Compression Toolkit to bring up the Voxware Compression Toolkit Properties dialog box.
  9. Click on the option (circle) next to "Do not use this codec". This should change the Status text to "Driver is disabled".
  10. Click OK to close the Voxware Compression Toolkit Properties dialog box.
  11. Click OK to close the Audio codecs Properties dialog box.
  12. Click OK to close the Sounds and Multimedia Properties dialog box.

Removing the Voxware Codec

Complete the following steps to remove the Voxware codec.

  1. From the Windows desktop, click on Start to bring up the Start menu.
  2. Click on Settings to bring up the Settings sub-menu.
  3. Click on Control Panel, to open the Control Panel.
  4. Double-click on Sounds and Multimedia to bring up the Sounds and Multimedia Properties dialog box.
  5. Click on the Hardware tab.
  6. Double-click on the Audio codecs option in the Devices list. This will bring up the Audio codecs Properties dialog box.
  7. Click on the Properties tab. This will bring up the Audio Compression codecs list.
  8. Click on the Voxware Compression Toolkit listing to select it.
  9. Click on the Remove button. This will bring up the Remove confirmation dialog box.
  10. Click on Yes to remove the codec.
  11. Click OK to close the Audio codecs Properties dialog box.
  12. Click OK to close the Sounds and Multimedia Properties dialog box.
