Billboard Sample

The Billboard sample shows how to use 2D "billboard" images in a 3D scene. Rather than rendering complex 3D geometry (such as a high-polygon tree model), billboarding renders a 2D image of the model and rotates it to always face the viewer. This technique is commonly used to render trees, clouds, smoke, explosions, and more.

The Billboard sample has a camera that flies around a 3D scene with a tree-covered hill. The trees look like 3D objects, but they are really 2D billboarded images that are rotated towards the eye point. The hilly terrain and the skybox (six-sided cube containing sky textures) are just objects loaded from .x files that are used for visual effect and are unrelated to the billboarding technique.

Overview

As the camera moves, the viewpoint changes; and as the viewpoint changes, a rotation matrix is generated to rotate the billboards about the y-axis so that they face the new viewpoint. Next, the trees are sorted; this is required for proper alpha blending, because billboards typically have some transparent pixels. The skybox, landscape, and trees are then rendered in the Render() method.

Note  The billboards in this sample are constrained to rotate about the y-axis only. Otherwise, the tree trunks would not seem to be attached to the ground. In a 3D flight simulator or space shooter, effects such as explosions typically use billboards that are not constrained to one axis.
