Getting Schedule Information

Players registered in a competition must be able to determine when their matches occur and who their opponents are. To provide this information, your title calls XOnlineCompetitionSearch and specifies the My Competitions query defined in XLAST. This is a standard query that XLAST creates automatically for you. The My Competitions query returns the list of events the player is scheduled to participate in for all competitions of the selected template type.

Alternatively, a player may discover the next event they're scheduled to participate in by viewing the topology of a competition. Your title obtain topology information by calling the XOnlineCompetitionTopologySingleElimination function. This function queries for all scheduled events in a given range. The title can then iterate through these events to find the event the player is scheduled to participate in next.

Note    Topology information is not available for competitions until registration has closed and the competition is no longer in the "pre-init" phase.

Even before a match has been played, the winner might have already been determined in a scheduled event. For instance, if a player has received a bye for the round, or if the opponent forfeited the event, the player wins automatically. In such cases, the player need not check in or play the event. The title should simply notify the player of the automatic win for the round. Then, as the next event becomes available, the player can proceed to play in the following round.
