Managing Competitions

Offering a large number and variety of competitions can give a game publisher the opportunity to immerse large numbers of customers in a title or group of titles. However, the volume of competitions can create management overhead for game publishers. Therefore, the Xbox Live competitions service offers two methods of managing competitions:

  • Competitions Managed by Publishers
  • Competitions Managed by Players

Competitions Managed by Publishers

Publisher-managed tournaments give the publisher total control over the elements of the competition, such as the timing of events, settling of disputes, and so forth. Publishers instantiate competitions from templates created and submitted to Xbox Live with the XLAST tool.

Publisher-managed tournaments can accommodate up to 1024 players.

Competitions Managed by Players

Competitions do not have to be managed by publishers. You can turn over the management of some or all of your competitions to players. Implementing player-managed tournaments can ensure much higher participation because it shifts the burden of managing tournaments onto empowered players, who typically outnumber the publisher staff. Players instantiate competitions from templates you create and submit to Xbox Live with the XLAST tool. Because players can only instantiate competition templates that you provide, you retain a large amount of control over the competition with much less overhead.

Player-managed competitions can accommodate up to 128 players. Also, as many as 3 competitions created by a given player may be active at the same time.
