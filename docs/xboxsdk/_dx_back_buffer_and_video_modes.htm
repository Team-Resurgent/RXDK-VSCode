Back Buffer Resolution and Video Modes

Games can always select a back buffer resolution of 640 × 480 which will work with NTSC, PAL, and HDTV display modes. If your back buffer size is specified to be 640 × 480, the front buffer will be scaled to match the native resolution for the display mode you have selected.

Video Mode Native Resolution
NTSC 640 × 480
NTSC 720 × 480
480p 720 × 480
720p 1280 × 720
1080i 1920 × 1080
PAL 640 × 576
PAL 720 × 576
PAL-60 640 × 480
PAL-60 720 × 480

To prevent this scaling, simply allocate a back buffer that is the same size as the native display mode. For example, for PAL output, allocate a frame buffer that is 640 × 576 pixels and no scaling will occur.

To enable widescreen, D3DPRESENTFLAG_WIDESCREEN must be ORed into the Flags member of D3DPRESENT_PARAMETERS. Widescreen affects the aspect ratio in the field of view and not the size of the back buffer.

To enable one of the progressive modes, such as 480p or 720p, D3DPRESENTFLAG_PROGRESSIVE needs to be ORed into the Flags member of D3DPRESENT_PARAMETERS.
