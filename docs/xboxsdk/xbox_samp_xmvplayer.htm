XMVPlayer Sample

The XMVPlayer sample demonstrates various ways of playing XMV movies in your game. XMV playback has a few principle variations:

  • Playing to a texture or to the overlay planes.
  • Using the packet interface to read the file, using packets to copy from memory, or letting XMV do the reading.
  • Using Play to play the entire movie or using GetNextFrame.
  • Unpacking to an RGB or YUV texture.
  • Playing full screen or on just part of the screen.

If these variations could be combined arbitrarily, you would have dozens of combinations. A few of the combinations don't make sense, however. For example, overlay planes are always YUV, Play always uses the overlay planes, and so on.

Many of these variations, such as using XMVDecoder_CreateDecoderForFile versus XMVDecoder_CreateDecoderForPackets, do not affect other aspects of playback, so the different variations can be mixed without difficulty.

This sample tries to show all sensible combinations of these possibilities.

When playing to an overlay plane this sample also demonstrates placing other graphics above the movie being played.
