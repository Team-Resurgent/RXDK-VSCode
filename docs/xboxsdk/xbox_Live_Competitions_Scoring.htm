Submitting Event Scores

After gameplay for an event session has completed, your title submits results to the service using XOnlineCompetitionSubmitResults. Every copy of your game involved in the event session must submit results for all players. The results are checked by the arbitration service, which selects the most credible results and forwards them to the competitions service. The competitions service progresses the winner to the next round and marks the loser as eliminated from the competition.

Note that when your title submits the results with XOnlineCompetitionSubmitResults, it can perform a quick check to help prevent cheating. Two of the XOnlineCompetitionSubmitResults function's parameters, pStatProcs and pAttributes, point to arrays of structures. These structures must identically match the arrays from every other player when compared at the binary level. Mismatched results are a signal to the arbitration service that one or more clients might be using an altered version of your title to submit bogus scores. This results in problematic sessions that may affect competition results. During development, titles should use debug code to compare and match submitted results and eliminate all cases where non-matching results are sent to the arbitration service for sessions that should have completed correctly.
