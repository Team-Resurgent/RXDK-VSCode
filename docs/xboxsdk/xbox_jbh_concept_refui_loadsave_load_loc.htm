GUI Model

Usability Issues with GUI

  1. Many participants failed to understand that there was storage on the console. It took them a few experiences with this screen to get it. This is why we chose to call the "Hard Disk" the hard disk rather than internal storage. The GUI was easier to understand with regard to internal storage than the List or Hybrid models because in those models the internal storage item looks like a header row for the list.
  2. The blue box that indicates selection was not obvious enough initially. Users had trouble understanding that the blue box showed that the internal storage on the console was selected.

    Recommendation: Selection color should be very obvious and it should not blend into the background.

  3. The gauges below the memory devices showed the free space on the device. This was counter to participant's expectations. All participants expected the gauges to show space used on the device. Several mentioned that the color of the gauge should also change to indicate an upcoming low storage condition.

    Recommendation: Have the gauges show the space used on the device.

    Recommendation: Have the gauges change color to yellow when the space available is less than 10 percent and to red when there isn't room on the device to save the game.

Participants liked the descriptive data that was displayed for each memory device. They appreciated knowing how much space was available. They also liked the ability to give custom names to each device.

Participants thought that this navigation screen was the easiest one on which to see what devices were connected to the system. They really liked the physical layout.

Hybrid Model

Usability Issues with Hybrid

  1. Again, it was difficult for participants to understand that there is storage on the console. In this model and in the List model the first device looks like a header row for the list.
  2. The gauges below the memory devices showed the free space on the device. This was counter to participant's expectations. All participants expected the gauges to show space used on the device. Several mentioned that the color of the gauge should also change to indicate an upcoming low storage condition.

    Recommendation: Have the gauges show the space used on the device.

    Recommendation: Have the gauges change color to yellow when the space available is less than 10 percent and to red when there isn't room on the device to save the game.

Participants thought that the navigation between devices was the easiest in this model. Again they liked the custom names of the devices and the descriptive data shown for each device.

List Model

This model doesn't show the available storage space for each device. Also, the location of memory units cannot be seen until they are highlighted.

Usability Issues with List

  1. As with all of the other models, participants found it difficult to understand storage was available on the console. In this model, the problem is particularly bad because it does not offer a graphic to differentiate between the hard disk and memory units.
  2. The gauges below the memory devices showed the free space on the device. This was counter to participant's expectations. All participants expected the gauges to show space used on the device. Several mentioned that the color of the gauge should also change to indicate an upcoming low storage condition.

    Recommendation: Have the gauges show the space used on the device.

    Recommendation: Have the gauges change color to yellow when the space available is less than 10 percent and to red when there isn't room on the device to save the game.

The List model is the most popular in today's video games. The List model was not popular with users even though it was something they were very familiar with in gameplay.
