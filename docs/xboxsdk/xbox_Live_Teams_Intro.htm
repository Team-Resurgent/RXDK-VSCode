Introduction to Team Competitions

Xbox Live teams can be used for a wide variety of purposes, including the following:

Sports games
Most sports games require the use of teams. With the Xbox Live teams service, your title can enable players to form teams that compete on a regular basis.
Clans
Many types of games can benefit by enabling players to form clans. Clans enable groups of players to compete with other clans in a given title. They can also compete across multiple titles. Either way, clans can help players build a group persona and mystique that is appropriate for the particular type of game. This enhances players' immersion into the title's milieu.
Building a sense of community
Titles generally retain more players if players can build a community around the game. Teams can enhance the community experience.

When a team is created, it must have at lease one initial member—the creator of the team. The creator of the team can recruit or expel members. At the time a team is created, the creator can specify a team's attributes.

The teams service places no limits on the number of team members in matches or competitions. The minimum requirement is that one team member be present to submit results. Game designers are free to add any requirements above and beyond this. Players can join multiple teams. The maximum size of any one team is 100 players.

The Xbox Live teams service focuses on the creation and management of teams. It has no API functions that deal with having teams participate in competitions. That is handled by XLAST and by the competitions service. To have a competition involving teams, you use XLAST to create a team competition template and submit it to Xbox Live. After it is approved, you or your players can instantiate an instance of the competition. In all aspects, your title handles the team competition just as it handles competitions between individuals. The primary difference between individual and team competitions from a programming point of view is that you pass the team's ID (rather than an individual's ID) to the XOnlineCompetitionXXXX functions.
